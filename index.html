About me

Postdoctoral Associate
NYU Biology

I have a background in academic research, with experience in pharmaceutical drug development, Molecular Biology, Genetics, and Genomics. With the advent of high-throughput experimentation in Biology, I transitioned from the wet lab to computational data analysis, particularly in the field of genomics.

Curently I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Hochwagen Lab at NYU. Our lab focuses on the study of meiosis, the specialized cell division required for sexual reproduction. My work involves the generation and analysis of high-throughput genome-wide sequencing data. I routinely use next-generation sequencing (NGS) technology and computational methods, including shell scripting, the programming languages R and Python, and open-source bioinformatics software.

Beyond Biology, I am interested in computational data analysis in general, or Data Science. When I'm not working on meiosis I like to dabble in Computer Science, Math, Statistics, and Machine Learning.
